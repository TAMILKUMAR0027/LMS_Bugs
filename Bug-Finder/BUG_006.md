## Preconditions

---
## Steps to Reproduce
1. Open any 'Edit' modal across modules (Course/Element/Service/Category).
2. Enter invalid data (blank, special characters, excessively long strings, duplicate names).
3. Submit the form.
---
## Expected Result
All Edit forms should enforce consistent field validation rules (required, format, length, duplicate/uniqueness) and block submission with clear inline error messages.
 
---
## Actual Result
Invalid/blank/duplicate data is accepted and saved successfully across all tested Edit forms, with no validation feedback to the user.
 
---

- [x] Always
- [ ] Intermittent

## Impact
This is a global/systemic finding: none of the Edit forms across the application (Course, Element, Service, Category, etc.) enforce field-level validation — such as required fields, character limits, allowed character sets, or duplicate checks. Any value, including blank, special-character-only, or malformed input, is accepted and saved, creating a significant data-integrity risk across the whole application.
